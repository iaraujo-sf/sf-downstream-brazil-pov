02

Optimizing margins, managing PPI parity, and executing FX hedging strategies in a volatile market.

03

Logistics (Logística)

Operating bases, pipelines, fleets, and mastering the critical last-mile delivery to thousands of stations.

04

Commercial Execution

Managing retail networks, large B2B accounts, and driving volume through disciplined sales operations.

05

Customer Operations

Providing credit, loyalty programs, fleet management, and TRR services to differentiate beyond the commodity.

A $76B Market Defined by Scale, Volatility, and Fragmentation

Massive market scale meets continental infrastructure challenges and extreme price volatility.

Massive Market Scale

~140 billion liters consumed annually. Market value exceeding $76 billion.

Continental Infrastructure

42,000+ retail stations and hundreds of bases across 3.27 million square miles.

Extreme Volatility

FX fluctuations, oil price swings, and frequently changing local tax regulations.

Fierce Fragmentation

Top 3 hold ~55% share. Remaining 45% fiercely contested by agile regional distributors.

Competitive Landscape

Three Giants Control >55% of a Highly Fragmented Market

The Big Three Dominance

Vibra, Raízen, and Ipiranga leverage massive scale, nationwide infrastructure, and strong brand equity — controlling approximately 66% of the branded market.

The "White-Flag" Challenge

Regional distributors and unbranded stations capture ~34% of the market by competing aggressively on price, lower overhead, and hyper-local agility.

Why Pricing Is Different in Brazil

A multi-variable equation unlike any other market. Pricing is not a commercial function — it's a treasury, tax, and supply function.

FactorImpact
PPI Abandonment (2022)Petrobras no longer follows International Parity Price — creates opacity and unpredictability in cost basis
FX ExposureBRL/USD volatility directly impacts import costs, with distributors absorbing timing risk
Tax LabyrinthICMS monofásico + PIS/COFINS + CIDE + state substituição tributária — each product/state is different
Biofuel MandatesEthanol (27%) and Biodiesel (15%) blend ratios shift effective cost per liter
Competitive PressureWhite-flag stations undercut branded networks on price — constant volume vs margin trade-off

The Pricing Operating Model

Five interconnected layers that determine margin capture — or margin leakage.

1

Cost Basis Construction

Petrobras gate price (or import landed cost) + biofuel blending cost + freight to terminal + tax burden per state/product

2

Market Intelligence

Competitor pricing monitoring (ANP data), regional demand elasticity, channel-specific willingness to pay

3

Margin Rules Engine

Floor margin by product/region/channel, target margin bands by customer tier, escalation triggers

4

Dynamic Pricing Execution

B2B contract pricing (indexed), spot pricing for TRR, promotional pricing for retail network

5

Performance Monitoring

Realized margin vs. target (daily), price-volume trade-off analysis, competitor response tracking

Fleet Management

Fleet Cards: The high-switching-cost volume guarantor for transport companies.

What Fleet Management Includes
